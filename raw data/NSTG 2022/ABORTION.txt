ABORTION
Introduction
One of the leading causes of morbidity and mortality among women in Nigeria. Defined as termination of pregnancy prior to age of viability (28 weeks of gestation, however, WHO regards age of viability as 20 weeks' gestation or 500-gm birth-weight). Early abortion occurs before 12weeks of gestation while late abortion occurs after 12weeks of gestation
Abortion could either be spontaneous abortion (miscarriage) or induced abortion

Types of Spontaneous Abortion
■ Threatened abortion
■ Inevitable abortion
■ Complete or incomplete abortion
■ Missed abortion
■ Recurrent abortion (Habitual Abortion)


CLINICAL FEATURES OF ABORTION 
Clinical Features
Threatened Abortion
■ Minimal vaginal bleeding with or without lower abdominal pains
■ Uterine cervical os closed
■ Anaemia

Inevitable abortion
■ Vaginal bleeding associated lower abdominal pains
■ Uterine cervical os dilated.
■ Drainage of liquor

Incomplete abortion (common gynaecological emergency)
■ Heavy vaginal bleeding,
■ Severe abdominal pain
■ Cervical os opened with tissue protruding in most cases

Missed abortion
■ Dead foetus in-utero before the age of viability
■ Regression of pregnancy symptoms
■ Uterine size may reduce

Recurrent Abortion
■ Three or more consecutive spontaneous abortions is considered recurrent abortion. Also referred to as Habitual abortion. Causative factors need to be evaluated and appropriate treatment be given.




INVESTIGATIONS OF ABORTION 
Investigations
■ PCV or Hb estimation
■ Urinalysis
■ Ultrasound scan to confirm if foetus is alive
■ Rhesus antigen status  
■ Grouping and cross-matching of blood  
■ Bed side clotting time in cases of missed abortion  
■ Clotting profile in cases of missed abortion  
■ Further investigations will depend upon the suspected cause  

TREATMENT OF ABORTION 
Treatment Goals  
■ Restore haemostasis  
■ Prevent/treat complications  
■ Provide health education and post abortion family planning  

Non-drug Treatment  
■ Bed rest  
■ Monitor vital signs  
■ Avoidance of strenuous activity and sexual intercourse (in threatened abortion)  
■ Nursing care  
■ Psychological support  
■ Personal hygiene  

Drug Treatment  
■ IV line with wide-bore cannula and Normal saline or 5% dextrose saline  
■ Manual Vacuum Aspiration (MVA) Or tabs misoprostol 600 μg orally (If pregnancy is less than 12 weeks).  
■ Infuse 40 units of oxytocin in a litre of normal saline or ringers lactate at 40 drops per minute to facilitate expulsion Or misoprostol (If pregnancy is more than 12 weeks)  
■ Haematinics or blood transfusion  
■ Prophylactic antibiotics for 5 days  
■ Analgesics  

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSES OF ABORTION 
Differential Diagnoses  
■ Ectopic pregnancy  
■ Hydatidiform mole  


COMPLICATIONS OF ABORTION 
Complications  
■ Endometritis, parametritis, peritonitis, haemorrhage, secondary infertility, chronic pelvic pain, dyspareunia.  
■ Induced abortion can be complicated by uterine perforation, bladder or intestinal injuries and shock  

PREVENTION OF ABORTION 
Prevention  
■ Promote personal and family understanding of basic reproductive health issues  
■ Provide access to efficient and effective higher levels of healthcare  
■ Review existing laws on abortion (based on the guidelines from SOGON) with a view to promoting and protecting the overall wellbeing of mother and the unborn child
