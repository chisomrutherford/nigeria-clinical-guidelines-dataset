ADENOID DISEASES
Introduction
A syndrome due to hyperplasia/hypertrophy of the adenoid tissue in the
nasopharynx.
Epidemiology
Commoner in children aged 2 - 6 years.

CLINICAL FEATURES OF ADENOID DISEASES 
・ Nasal obstruction and mouth-breathing
・ Snoring at night
・ Obstructive sleep apnoea
・ Progressive deafness due to secretory otitis media
・ Chronic infection of adenoid tissue may be present
・ Symptom may subside spontaneously as adenoid regress and become
atrophic with age

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSES OF ADENOID DISEASES 
・ Allergic rhinitis
・ Sinusitis
・ Otitis media

COMPLICATIONS OF ADENOID DISEASES
・ Sinusitis
・ Recurrent otitis media
・ Pneumonitis

INVESTIGATIONS OF ADENOID DISEASES 
・ X-ray of nasopharynx
・ X-ray of sinuses and chest
・ Nasal endoscopy

TREATMENT OF ADENOID DISEASES 
Treatment Goals
・ To improve nasopharyngeal airway and nasal breathing
・ To treat concurrent infection
Non-drug Treatment
・ Adenoidectomy in severe cases
Drug Treatment
・ Decongestants
- Psuedoephedrine syrup
- 6-12 years: 30 mg (5 mL of syrup) orally 8 hourly;
- 2-5 years: 15 mg (2.5 mL) 4-8 hourly
Or:
- Ephedrine nasal drops (0.5%); Instill into nostrils twice daily and at
night time
・ Antibiotics
- Amoxicillin syrup 125 - 250 mg orally 8 hourly for 5-7 days
