CHOLESTASIS OF PREGNANCY 
Introduction
Uncommon, in the order of 1:2,000 pregnancies. Presents commonly in late third trimester, after 36 weeks. Clinically significant because of its association with intra-uterine foetal growth retardation (IUGR) and intra-uterine foetal death (IUFD) (mechanism unclear). It is not as a rule associated with maternal Complications

CLINICAL FEATURES OF CHOLESTASIS OF PREGNANCY 
Clinical Features
■ Generalized pruritus
■ Decreased foetal movements
■ Upper abdominal pain
■ Dark urine
■ Steatorrhea
■ Occasionally there is jaundice (particularly in the later stages of the disease)

INVESTIGATIONS OF CHOLESTASIS OF PREGNANCY 
Investigations
■ Liver function tests:
  · Mildly deranged
  · Serum bilirubin and bile salts may be elevated

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSES OF CHOLESTASIS OF PREGNANCY 
Differential Diagnoses
■ Viral hepatitis
■ Early HELLP syndrome
■ Acute fatty liver

TREATMENT OF CHOLESTASIS OF PREGNANCY 
Treatment
■ Careful maternal follow-up with LFTs
■ Foetal surveillance: by growth (serial ultrasound biometry) and wellbeing (cardio-tocographic foetal) monitoring
■ If all is well induce at 38 weeks
■ Management of associated pruritus: (Difficult to manage)
  · Topical agents offer little help
  · Colestyramine : binds bile salts and fat soluble vitamins
  · Vitamin K : To decrease bleeding tendencies
· Antihistamines: May offer brief respite
· Ursodeoxycholic acid and colestyramine (orally) decrease itching and normalize liver function
· Adult: 10 - 15 mg/kg daily in 2 - 4 divided doses
· Child 1 month -18 years: 10 - 15 mg/kg twice daily; total dose may be given in 3 divided doses

Recurrence
Quite high
Prognosis
Good with complete recovery in days to weeks
