CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE
Introduction
A heart defect that occurs during the formation of the heart in utero could be
fatal (i.e. causes intrauterine death, or death at anytime afterwards). An
important cause of perinatal morbidity/mortality.
• Classified as cyanotic or acyanotic

CLINICAL FEATURES OF CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE
• Will depend on the type of the defect:
- Mild defects go unnoticed
• Stunted growth
• Cyanosis
• Failure to thrive
• Heart murmurs

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSES OF CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE
• Rheumatic heart disease
• Endomyocardial fibrosis

COMPLICATIONS OF CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE
• Embolic phenomena
• Cardiac failure

INVESTIGATIONS OF CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE
• FBC and differentials
• Electrolyte, Urea and Creatinine
• Chest radiograph
• Electrocardiograhy
• Foetal echocardiography
• Angiography

TREATMENT GOALS OF CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE
• Relieve symptoms
• Treat the definitive defect(s)

TREATMENT OF CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE
Non-drug Treatment
Low salt diet

Drug Treatment
Treatment of cardiac failure if present with digoxin, diuretics and potassium
supplements

Supportive Measures
Oxygen
Counselling
Prevention
• Pre-conception nutrition education
• Antenatal care
• Genetic counselling
