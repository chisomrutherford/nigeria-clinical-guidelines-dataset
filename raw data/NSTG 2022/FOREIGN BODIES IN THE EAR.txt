FOREIGN BODIES IN THE EAR
Introduction
A common presentation in ENT emergency practice; commoner in children
than adults. Children may inadvertently insert various objects (beads, plastic
toys, seeds etc) into their ears while playing. Live insects may also crawl into
the ear in adults/children

CLINICAL FEATURES OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE EAR
・ May be asymptomatic
・ Mild pain; sensation of blockage in older children
・ Object usually seen with good light in the ear canal

INVESTIGATIONS OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE EAR
・ Ear Endoscopy
・ Xray of petromastoid
・ CT Scan of petromastoid

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSES OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE EAR
・ Impacted wax
・ Otitis externa

COMPLICATIONS OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE EAR
・ Otitis externa
・ Perforation of tympanic membrane from attempts at removal

TREATMENT OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE EAR
Treatment Goals
・ Remove object expeditiously without damage to ear structures or causing
undue pain to patient
Non-drug Treatment
・ Removal by ear syringing
- Removal with appropriate hook, or alligator forceps
- Examination and removal under anaesthesia if difficult in the clinic
Prevention
・ Vigilant supervision of young children
