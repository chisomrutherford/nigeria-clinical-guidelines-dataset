FOREIGN BODIES IN THE NOSE AND RHINOLITHS 
Introduction
Typically result in foul smelling unilateral nasal discharge. Some inorganic
objects may (after long periods) become coated by hard calcific deposits and
become known as rhinoliths. High index of suspicion is important. Once care
giver insists on foreign body inhalation (symptomatic or asymptomatic), it
must be fully investigated.

CLINICAL FEATURES OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE NOSE AND RHINOLITHS 
・ Often no indication or symptom
・ May be noticed accidentally by parent
・ Later, complaints of foul purulent unilateral nasal discharge of unknown
origin

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSES OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE NOSE AND RHINOLITHS
・ Acute or chronic rhinitis/sinusitis
・ Nasal growth/polyp

COMPLICATIONS OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE NOSE AND RHINOLITHS
Secondary infection: rhinosinusitis

INVESTIGATIONS OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE NOSE AND RHINOLITHS 
・ Nasal endoscopy
・ Radiograph of nose: for metallic or radio-opaque objects
・ CT Scan of the nose and paranasal sinuses when missile injuries are
involved

TREATMENT OF FOREIGN BODIES IN THE NOSE AND RHINOLITHS 
Treatment Goals
・ Remove object safely with little discomfort to patient
Non-drug Treatment
・ Careful removal with appropriate hook or forceps
・ Removal under anaesthesia as necessary
Prevention
・ Vigilant supervision of young children
