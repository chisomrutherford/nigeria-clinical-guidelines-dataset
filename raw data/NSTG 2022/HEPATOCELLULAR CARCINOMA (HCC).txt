HEPATOCELLULAR CARCINOMA (HCC)
Introduction
The most common primary liver malignancy. HCC has a huge burden on West
Africa due to the high endemicity of a number of risk factors like hepatitis B, C,
and HIV. In Nigeria, middle aged individuals, mainly males, are more
commonly affected and they present late.
Risk factors
・ Cirrhosis
・ Hepatitis B and C infection
・ alcohol intake
・ diabetes
・ obesity
・ male sex
・ aflatoxins
・ metabolic and genetic diseases (eg haemochromatosis, Wilson's disease, α
1 anti-trypsin deficiency, tyrosinemia, porphyrias)
・ cigarette smoking.

CLINICAL FEATURES OF HEPATOCELLULAR CARCINOMA (HCC)
Clinical Features
・ Elective: Painful right hypochondrial mass, weight loss, early satiety,
fatigue, ascites
・ Emergency: Coagulopathy, encephalopathy, acute abdomen (rupture and
haemorrhage)

INVESTIGATIONS OF HEPATOCELLULAR CARCINOMA (HCC)
Investigations
・ Diagnosis: Liver biopsy
・ Radiologic: Abdominopelvic USS, Abdominal CT
・ Biochemical: LFT, and Serum proteins, Electrolyte, Urea and Creatinine,
Tumour markers (α-fetoprotein, des-gamma-carboxy prothrombin, C-
reactive protein)
・ Haemotological: FBC

TREATMENT OF HEPATOCELLULAR CARCINOMA (HCC)
Treatment
・ Treatment modality depends on tumour size, location, extrahepatic spread,
and underlying liver function
・ Surgery: Liver resection - early stages, solitary lesions; arterial ligation for
non-resectable lesions
・ Others: Systemic chemotherapy, percutaneous ethanol injection,
radiotherapy, radiofrequency ablation
Prevention/Screening
・ Hepatitis B screening and vaccination
・ Abdominal USS/CT
・ Serological markers: α-fetoprotein
