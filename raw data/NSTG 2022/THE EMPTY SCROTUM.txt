THE EMPTY SCROTUM
Introduction
A clinical situation in which the testis is absent from the scrotum. May be
bilateral or unilateral
Aetiology
・ Undescended testis
・ Ectopic testis
・ Retractile testis
・ Absent (vanishing) testis
- Atrophic testis
- Surgical removal (for treatment of other conditions)

CLINICAL FEATURES OF THE EMPTY SCROTUM
Clinical Features
・ Absence of one or both testes from the scrotum
・ Pain from trauma to the testis
・ Infertility (in adulthood)
・ Atrophic testis
・ Inguinal hernia may be present on the affected side

COMPLICATIONS OF THE EMPTY SCROTUM
Complications
・ Torsion of the spermatic cord
・ Trauma to the testis
・ Malignancy
・ Infertility

INVESTIGATIONS OF THE EMPTY SCROTUM
Investigations
・ Urinary 17-ketosteroids, gonadotropins
・ Serum testosterone
・ Ultrasonography
・ Computed tomography
・ Laparoscopy
・ Magnetic Resonance Imaging

TREATMENT OF THE EMPTY SCROTUM
Treatment
・ Hormone therapy:
- Human chorionic gonadotropin 1,500 units/week intramuscularly, for a
total of 9 injections
- Applicable only to special cases
・ Surgical treatment: In those with undescended testes bring testis down and
fix it in the scrotum
