VERTIGO
Introduction
Vertigo is hallucination of movement (patient feels s/he is moving or the
environment is moving) It is a symptom/feature of vestibular organ disfunction
The commonest type is known as benign paroxysmal positiobal
vertigo(BPPV), which results when the otoliths in the posterior semicircular
canal is disorganized /reoriented in wrong position.
The trigger is usually sudden movement of the head.May also result from
excessive wax, infections, tumours of inner ear (acoustic neuroma), head
injuries and metabolic causes including cardiovascular disease.

CLINICAL FEATURES OF VERTIGO 
・ Sensation of movement(e.g. spinning), exaggerated dizziness
・ Nausea, sometimes vomiting, tinnitus(ringing in the ear) may be present.

DIFFERENTIAL DIAGNOSES OF VERTIGO 
・ Meniere's disease
・ Vestibulitis
・ Acoustic neuroma

COMPLICATIONS OF VERTIGO 
・ Poor quality of life
・ Suicidal tendencies

INVESTIGATIONS OF VERTIGO 
・ Audiologic tests - Pure Tone Audiogram (PTA), Tympanometry,
・ Automated Brain Response Audiometry (where possible)
・ CT scan in complicated cases
・ MRI in suspected inner ear tumours
・ Ear Endoscopy

TREATMENT OF VERTIGO 
Treatment Goals
・ Identify cause and treat accordingly
Non-drug Treatment (mainstay of treatment)
・ Canalithic Repositioning Manoeuvres e,g.
- Epley's manoeuvre
- Sermon manoeuvre
Drug Treatment (symptomatic relief)
・ Labyrinthine sedatives
- Beta histine 16 mg twice daily
- Cinnarizine 15 - 30 mg twice daily
・ Antiemetics
- Promethazine
- Adult: 25 mg orally at night, increased to 25 mg twice daily if necessary
or, 10 - 20 mg 8 - 12 hourly
- Child:
- Not recommended to children under 2 years
- 5 - 10 years: 10 - 25 mg orally daily in 1-2 divided doses;
- 2 - 5 years: 5 - 15 mg daily in 1 - 2 divided doses
